perf(audio): stop playback-time decoded scrub buffer thrash

During playback with many audio lanes, opportunistic scrub-buffer
warmups re-decoded ~100MB WAV AudioBuffers every cycle: the 192MB/3-
entry cache cannot hold stacked 5-minute stems, evicted buffers were
re-requested immediately, and successful-but-unretainable decodes had
no backoff. Measured ~60MB/s heap growth and 19fps UI during playback.

- proxyFrameCache.warmScrubAudioBuffer(): explicit opportunistic
  warmup entry point with a 5s per-file backoff set when a decode is
  not retained (incl. runtime admission rejection) or a cached buffer
  is evicted; getAudioBuffer() keeps demand semantics for stem mixer,
  export, and scrub playback
- retryTime semantics converted to next-allowed-attempt-at
- warmup call sites in AudioTrackSyncManager and
  audioTrackRuntimeElements now opt in explicitly and never warm while
  playback is active (flag threaded from FrameContext instead of a new
  store read, keeping the getState access policy ceiling)
- element preloads (preloadAudioProxy) unchanged

Implemented by a Codex worker from a reviewed packet; orchestrator
added the opt-in flag threading, test-setup mock, and verification.
Verified live: heap stable during playback (was +60MB/s), long tasks
35 -> 5 per 10s window, decode loop gone from LoAF attribution.
